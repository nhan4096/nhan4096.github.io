Welcome to A Bootleg SCP Collection! Now also known as Intelization for various reasons.

As it says on the tin. These are SCPs that I wrote, for your enjoyment.

A few precautions: The SCP numbers do not correspond to actual SCP entries on the SCPWiki. The SCP entries will be obviously subpar to those on the SCPWiki, as I'm not an experienced writer. Finally, these SCPs are completely unaffiliated with the SCPWiki, as I am underage and cannot post there.

SCP-9016: "Câm mồm!"

Do we need to divert a major part of our budget towards this? I do not think humanity is ending anytime soon — sure, all of us are mute now, but that isn't fatal.

Author's note: Recommended for starters; heavy philosophical content.

SCP-9030: Con bì trừ điểm quang phổ vì quá ồn con :(

There was a very rude man at the intersection today. He yelled at me for "not seeing the red light". It wasn't very clear to me at first, since the red light was dimly lit.

Author's note: Recommended for starters; definitely more emotional. Slightly weaker than my previous work.

SCP-9160: End of the Human Epoch

Our content optimizes for maximal viewer retention and emotional impact, incorporating modern and contemporary horror, true crime, detective fiction artifacts to provide our readers with a lasting memory.

Author's note: Also slightly weaker than my previous works.

SCP-9019: PROTOTYPE

Author's note: My weakest SCP yet, as it was heavily rushed. Not proud of this one. Avoid.

SCP-9595: Erstwhile / Praeteritum, Non Praeteritum / "Sao mấy bà cô lập tui?!"

I drift like a ghost across numerous cliques. They said I was a good friend. They said they enjoyed my company. Not more than others, apparently.

Author's note: My best work so far. Be warned as it is significantly longer than any of my other pieces, clocking in at approximately 10000 words. For this reason and also for its content, probably not recommended for first-time reading.

SCP-9161: And I Still Feel It.

Then, there's the temperature. It's frigid. That's blue. What your skin feels has a color.

Author's note: Recommended for starters. Second-best work by me, maybe? Surprisingly short (~4000 words), but very emotional.
